Interlaken is a small city in the Bernese Highlands region of central Switzerland. Located between two alpine lakes (Lake Brienz (Brienzersee) and Lake Thun (Thunersee)), Interlaken is a popular base camp for outdoor sports and travel in the surrounding Bernese Oberland Alps. Interlaken itself is a superb holiday destination. This runs every 30 min to Wilderswil and Zweilütschinen, where it divides. The rear portion runs to Grindelwald, which is nowadays the main way to reach Jungfraujoch perched atop the mountains. The front portion continues to Lauterbrunnen, which has connections to Wengen, Kleine Scheidegg, Gimmelwald, Mürren and the top of the Schilthorn. Eurail passes are now valid for travel beyond Interlaken, to Lauterbrunnen, Grindelwald and Mürren and all BOB stations.
